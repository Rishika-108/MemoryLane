Memory Lane Privacy & Data Protection

<h1>Privacy Policy</h1>

<p class="subtitle">
Effective Date: May 10, 2026<br/>
Applicable to the Memory Lane browser extension and web platform.
</p>

<div class="section">
<h2>Introduction</h2>

<p>
Memory Lane ("we", "our", or "us") is a personal knowledge and memory
assistant designed to help users save, organize, summarize, and retrieve
meaningful web content.
</p>

<p>
This Privacy Policy explains what information is collected,
how it is processed, and how users maintain control over
their information when using Memory Lane.
</p>

<div class="highlight">
Memory Lane only operates after user authentication and
when explicitly enabled by the user.
</div>
</div>

<div class="section">
<h2>Information We Collect</h2>

<p>
When users manually save content or enable Auto Capture,
Memory Lane may collect:
</p>

<ul>
<li>Webpage URL</li>
<li>Page title</li>
<li>Relevant webpage text content used for summarization</li>
<li>User-selected text (when applicable)</li>
<li>YouTube metadata including video title, URL, and channel name</li>
<li>PDF text content intentionally captured by the user</li>
<li>Timestamps related to captures</li>
<li>Engagement signals such as time spent and scroll depth</li>
<li>Authentication tokens required to connect the extension to a Memory Lane account</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="section">
<h2>Manual Capture</h2>

<p>
Users may manually save content using the Memory Lane browser extension.
When manually triggered, Memory Lane may extract relevant webpage or document content in order to generate AI-powered summaries and memory organization.
</p>
</div>

<div class="section">
<h2>Auto Capture</h2>

<p>
Auto Capture is optional and disabled by default.
Users may enable it after signing into their Memory Lane account.
</p>

<p>
Memory Lane only performs automatic capture after meaningful engagement thresholds are met, including:
</p>

<ul>
<li>Minimum time spent on page (approximately 15 seconds)</li>
<li>Minimum scroll engagement (approximately 20%)</li>
<li>Minimum content threshold (approximately 200+ characters of readable content)</li>
</ul>

<div class="highlight">
Memory Lane does not automatically capture every webpage indiscriminately.
Capture occurs only after engagement thresholds are met.
</div>
</div>

<div class="section">
<h2>Excluded Content</h2>

<p>
For privacy and safety reasons, Memory Lane excludes certain categories of content from capture.
</p>

<ul>
<li>Banking and financial websites</li>
<li>Password and login fields</li>
<li>Email inboxes</li>
<li>Private messaging and social media direct messages</li>
<li>Private or restricted account areas</li>
<li>Incognito/private browsing sessions</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="section">
<h2>How Information Is Used</h2>

<p>
Collected information may be used to:
</p>

<ul>
<li>Generate AI-powered summaries and insights</li>
<li>Save and organize memories</li>
<li>Enable semantic search and retrieval</li>
<li>Improve learning retention and productivity features</li>
<li>Synchronize captures across devices and user sessions</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="section">
<h2>AI Processing</h2>

<p>
To provide summarization and memory organization features,
relevant webpage or document text may be securely transmitted
to Memory Lane backend services for AI-powered processing.
</p>

<p>
This processing may include summarization, categorization,
embedding generation, and retrieval optimization.
</p>
</div>

<div class="section">
<h2>Data Storage</h2>

<p>
Memory Lane may store information both locally in the user's browser
and securely on backend systems associated with the user's account.
</p>

<p>
Stored information may include summaries, metadata,
captured content, and memory organization data.
</p>
</div>

<div class="section">
<h2>Permissions</h2>

<p>
The Memory Lane extension may request browser permissions required for core functionality, including:
</p>

<ul>
<li>tabs and activeTab — to identify pages selected by the user</li>
<li>scripting — to extract content for saving and summarization</li>
<li>storage — to maintain login sessions and preferences</li>
<li>host permissions — to process supported webpages selected or meaningfully engaged with by users</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="section">
<h2>Third-Party Services</h2>

<p>
Memory Lane may use trusted cloud, database,
authentication, hosting, and AI service providers
required to deliver core platform functionality.
</p>
</div>

<div class="section">
<h2>User Control</h2>

<p>
Users may:
</p>

<ul>
<li>Enable or disable Auto Capture</li>
<li>Disconnect the extension by logging out</li>
<li>Disable the extension at any time</li>
</ul>

<p>
Memory Lane functionality requires account authentication.
Without login, capture features remain unavailable.
</p>
</div>

<div class="section">
<h2>Analytics</h2>

<p>
Memory Lane does not sell browsing activity or user data for advertising purposes.
We do not use captured content for behavioral advertising.
</p>
</div>

<div class="section">
<h2>Security</h2>

<p>
Reasonable technical and organizational safeguards are implemented
to protect user information, including secure transmission practices,
access control systems, and protected backend infrastructure.
</p>

<p>
However, no internet transmission or storage method can be guaranteed
to be fully secure.
</p>
</div>

<div class="section">
<h2>Children's Privacy</h2>

<p>
Memory Lane is not intended for children under 13 years of age.
</p>
</div>

<div class="section">
<h2>Changes to This Policy</h2>

<p>
We may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes
in technology, legal obligations, or platform functionality.
</p>

<p>
Continued use of Memory Lane after updates constitutes acceptance
of the revised policy.
</p>
</div>
